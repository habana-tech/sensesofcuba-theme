Whenever you access our webpage your browser automatically submits your current IP address as well as which browser you are using. This data together with the time of your request, the status and the page requested is stored in our logs for a few days in order to identify and rectify error conditions or security issues (Art. 6 Nr. 1 lit. f GDPR). In accordance to Art. 28 GDPR these processors are bound by contracts to only use the data for our purposes, they are not allowed to use it for their own purposes. Besides this strict contract we also verified whether their IT security is up to the state of the art.

In case you use one of our contact forms or decide to send us an email, we will receive your personal data such as your email address and your name. If we are required by e.g. taxation laws to keep a record of this transaction we will store the data as long as we are legally required to (Art. 6 Nr. 1 lit. c GDPR). If there is no legal requirement we will delete the data as soon as we know your enquiry was answered successfully.

We will not transfer this data to a third party except we are forced to by legal requirements, court orders etc. We might also use processors as mentioned above.

Our web site provides links to other pages. Naturally for pages run by a third party we cannot explain their privacy policy. Please read their individual rules carefully.

When linking social media, such as Facebook, Google+ and Twitter, we use the “double click solution” provided by heise.de. Due to this your data is only transmitted to these providers once you actively decide to do so. It is therefore your own responsibility to verify their privacy policy.

To provide some functionality our website uses cookies which we are not using for tracking, except for cookies provided by Google Analytics (see below). If feasible both from a technical as well as logical perspective you have the right to data portability where we have to provide the data stored about you in a standard format in order for another party to import it into their systems.

Information on Google Analytics

If you have given your consent, Google Analytics, a web analysis service of Google LLC (“Google”) is used on this website. The use includes the “Universal Analytics” operating mode. This makes it possible to assign data, sessions and interactions across multiple devices to a pseudonymous user ID and thus analyse a user's activities across devices. This data protection notice on Google Analytics is provided by www.intersoft-consulting.de.

Google Analytics uses “cookies”, which are text files placed on your computer, to help the website analyse how users interact with the site. The information generated by the cookie about your use of this website is usually transferred to a Google server in the USA and stored there. However, if IP anonymisation is activated on this website, Google will reduce your IP address within Member States of the European Union or in other states party to the Agreement on the European Economic Area beforehand. Only in exceptional cases will the full IP address be transmitted to a Google server in the USA and shortened there. We would like to point out that on this website Google Analytics has been extended to include IP anonymisation in order to ensure anonymous collection of IP addresses (so-called IP masking). The IP address transmitted by your browser in the context of Google Analytics is not merged with other Google data. For more information on terms of use and data protection, please visit https://www.google.com/analytics/terms/gb.html or https://policies.google.com/?hl=en

Purposes of the Processing

On behalf of the operator of this website, Google will use this information to evaluate your use of the website, to compile reports on website activity and to provide the website operator with other services related to website and Internet use.
